
You're presented with a big pile of reading material. Reading every book/article/talk from front to back isn't going to get you what you need fast enough.

#### 1. Summarize as quickly as possible

Use every tool at your disposal. Search the internet for reviews, summaries, and critiques. Only note new points you encounter from each source. 

**Output**
Notes on other people's takeaways where you found them.


#### 2. Edit your summaries

What are the central points? What are the interesting gems? What's the jargon?

**Output**
One summary file per text.

#### 3. Question the material

What do you want to know more about? What do you disagree with? What challenges your ideas?

**Output**
List of questions per text relevant to you.

#### 4. Crack the book

Read parts only for answers to your questions. Add additional questions as you learn more. Repeat until questions exhausted. 

**Output**
Notes on questions answered and questions unanswered.

#### 5. Own it

Triage unanswered questions: now, later, never. Expand research to unanswered questions. 

**Output**
In your own words, summary + questions + answers + further research.
