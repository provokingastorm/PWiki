This document contains notes, strategies and sources for acquiring movies, TV show, and other video media. 

## Notes From Nils
These are notes given by Nils over e-mail on February 17th 2025. 

### Online Retail Sources
These are notes from Nils about online retailers where you can purchase movies. 

#### **Shout Factory**
Boutique Blu-ray label. Plenty of Roger Corman and John Carpenter films. The Friday the 13th collection is currently on sale for $89 (12qty movies). I usually wait for sales unless it’s something I can’t live without. They usually have a theme for the sale (i.e. horror or John Carpenter or Roger Corman or going-out-of-print.) Fun multipacks for obscure films.

#### **Vinegar Syndrome**
Boutique Blu-ray label. Quite a bit of obscure stuff. Some releases from major studios (i.e. Cloak & Dagger, Road House). Quality of the movies themselves can vary wildly on this label but the transfers/masters they use are always top-notch and in some cases are some of the absolute best. Big sales are Black Friday and half way to black Friday (May). Some lesser sales throughout the year. Some of the best art/packaging as well.

#### **Severin Films**
Boutique Blu-ray label. Mostly obscure stuff. I usually wait for sales unless it’s something I can’t live without.

#### **Hamilton Books**
Many brand new DVDs for $2 and Blu-rays for $5. Quite a few mainstream movies. Stuff from boutique brands make an appearance here as well.

#### **Blu-ray.com (affiliate links to Amazon)**
Lists deals and so forth on a daily basis. Reviews of Blu-rays are usually helpful. Doesn’t really link to other sites besides Amazon so keep that in mind when viewing availability/pricing.

#### **eBay**
Every once in a while I’ll buy a VHS tape or a steelbook from here. If I can get a DVD/Blu-ray on eBay at the same/similar price as Amazon, I’ll try to order from eBay. How much of my money does Jeff Bezos need? Some eBay sellers are much better at packing physical media as well. This can of course vary from seller to seller. If something is out of print and going for high prices, I’ll just wait for a rerelease or try to find a cheaper version.

#### **Mill Creek**
Used to be able to find this stuff at Menards, but not at my local store. Has plenty of those compilation (12-movie, 50-movie, 100-movie) sets. Quality of the movies and the quality of the releases can vary wildly. The titles in the compilation sets often overlap but if you get the sets cheap enough it’s not a big deal. I really enjoy the VHS-style slipcovers on some of their releases.

#### **Walmart** 
Surprisingly my go to place for physical media (in-store) these days. Exclusive slipcovers, exclusive steelbooks. Cheap DVDs ($5). Some cheap Blu-rays ($7). Varies seasonally (Home Alone and Christmas Vacation show up in November).

#### **Target** 
Target doesn’t seem to sell DVDs anymore.

#### **Thrift stores** (Goodwill, Salvation Army)
Usually $2 for DVDs, $5 for Blu-rays. VHS tapes for a dollar or less. VHS tapes seem harder to find than even a few years ago. I suspect some thrift stores are just tossing them.

#### **Stuff Store** 
Iowa only, I believe.

#### **Dollar Tree**
Used to get quite a few good movies here (Robocop on Blu-ray, The Road Warrior on Blu-ray). Not sure if they sell Blu-rays/DVDs anymore. Haven’t seen any there recently. Not a bad spot to get replacement cases either (can just toss the DVD/insert from any movie not worth owning since you only paid $1.25).

#### **Best Buy**
Doesn’t sell movies anymore.

### Other Stuff
These are miscellaneous notes from Nils about acquiring video. 

- **Caps a holic** is probably the only site I truly trust for A to B testing. Reviews will always vary wildly on whether or not a release has good picture quality. This site has yet to steer me wrong.

- **Blu-ray.com:** has quite a few reviews. Active forums. Just don’t get too caught up in reviews. Some people will start a riot if film grain is missing or present or if the color timing doesn’t match what they believe they saw in the theater 40 years ago.

- **The Digital Bits:** long-standing physical media news site. Insider industry news. Still good for updates although I find their reviews on picture quality to be too lenient/forgiving for my taste.

- There are still some **print magazines** that discuss physical media. I can’t remember the titles off the top of my head. Barnes and Noble and other stores carry them. Some of the magazines talk about the movies themselves too much and gloss over the audio/picture quality and extras too much.
