Here are some notes and ideas of how to clean with bleach to disinfect things and surfaces in your home.
## Starting Tips and Safety Guide

 - Never clean with Bleach at full strength. Always dilute it. 
 - Make sure to have good ventilation. 
 - Always use gloves when cleaning with bleach. 
 - Always pre-clean surfaces to remove dirt and dust before cleaning with bleach.
 - Wear clothes that can be damaged. Bleach will remove color from clothing.
 - Bleach is best used fresh. Don't let the bleach solution sit for more than a day. 

## Bathroom Cleaning

Here are tips on cleaning your bathroom with bleach. 

**Sinks & Countertops**

 - Combine 1 gallon of cool water in a bucket mixed with 1/3 cup of bleach.
 - After first cleaning all surfaces, wet a towel in the bleach solution, making sure to ring it out. 
 - To disinfect the countertops, leave the solution on the surface wet for 6 minutes.
 - After disinfecting, use a towel, soaked in water only, to wipe off the bleach.
 - Finally, use a dry, clean microfiber towel to remove any remaining water spots. 

**Toilet**

 - Use the exact same process on toilets as the sinks/countertops. 
 - Definitely let the bleach solution sit for 6 minutes. 
 - You can use the solution on the floor around the toilet as well.
 - You can dispose of the bleach solution in a toilet when done.

**Shower**

 - Create the solution similar to the Sink section. 
 - Let the bleach solution sit for 10 minutes, instead of 6 minutes. 
 - Use a non-scratch scrub brush, or an old toothbrush, for tile and grout. 
 - Rise the surfaces afterwards with a towel, then buff with a dry microfiber cloth. 

## Kitchen Cleaning

**Sink & Countertops**

 - Same process as the bathroom. 
 - Instead of using a bucket, you can use the sink itself to hold the gallon of water and bleach. 

**Handles**

 - You can clean high-touch handles with bleach, such as the microwave handle, refridgerator handle, door handles, light switches etc. 

**Plastic Cutting Boards**

 - Useful for plastic cutting boards used to cut meat. 
 - Find a container that can hold the cutting board. Use a sink if necessary. 
 - Combine 1 gallon of water plus 2 teaspoons of bleach to make the solution.
 - Soak the cutting board for 2 minutes. Use a heavier item to help keep the cutting board submerged. 
 - After 2 minutes are up, leave the cutting board out to air dry. 

**Reusable Water Bottles**

 - First, do a quick rinse of the water bottle with soap. 
 - Create a solution of 1 gallon of water to 2 teaspoons of bleach. You may need to double the solution to fully submerge the water bottle. 
 - Soak the water bottle for 2 minutes. 
 - After the 2 minutes are up, drain the water bottle and leave it out to air dry.
