These are instruction on cleaning blinds, whether they're plastic, wood, cellular or fabric blinds.

## Pre-Cleaning the Blinds
Before beginning the cleaning, there are a few steps to follow.

 1. Dust the front of the blinds using the [[#Recommended Dusting Methods]] for the blind type.
 2. Dust the back of the blinds.

### Recommended Dusting Methods
The method of dusting will vary based on which type of blinds you're cleaning.

**Plastic or Wood blinds**
Use a disposable Swifter duster. They are easy to use. The dusters will capture the dust before the dust falls to the floor. 

**Cellular or Fabric blinds**
Use a handheld vacuum with brush attachment to *slowly* vacuum the blinds. Swifter duster won't be effective at pulling the dust out. Be sure to be gentle with vacuum to avoid damaging the blinds.

## Cleaning the Blinds
It's recommended to use a three microfiber towel system. The first microfiber towel is dipped in the cleaning solution. The second microfiber towel is dipped in clean water. The third towel is a dry towel to wipe away any liquid from the previous microfiber towels. Remember to be gentle when cleaning to avoid damage. Follow these step to finish cleaning the blinds.

 1. Prepare the [[#Cleaning Solution]] based on the type of blinds you're cleaning.
 2. Wipe the blinds down using the first towel (dipped in the cleaning solution).
	 1. Make sure to wipe down the cords the open the blinds, if present.
	 2. Make sure to wipe down any strings holding the blinds together, if present.
 3. Wipe the blinds down using the second towel (dipped in water).
 4. Wipe the blinds down using the third towel (dry).

### Cleaning Solution
The cleaning products are typically heavily concentrated. Cleaning directly with the solution can cause an unpleasant, sticky residue. This residue can actually hold onto dirt making your blinds dirtier faster. Therefore, it's best to dilute the recommended cleaning product with water. The solution will depend on the type of blinds you're cleaning. Follow the manta "less is more." 

**Plastic blinds**
 1. Fill a bucket with a gallon or so of water.
 2. Add a few squirts of Dawn dish soap to the bucket. 
 3. Mix the solution.

**Wood blinds**
 1. Fill a bucket with a gallon or so of water.
 2. Add a cap full of Murphy Oil wood cleaner to the bucket. 
 3. Mix the solution.

**Cellular blinds**
 1. Use the handheld vacuum with brush attachment to clean the blinds. 
 2. Spot clean with a *minimum* amount of Dawn dish soap mixed with water. 
 3. Call a professional if more effort needed. 

## Heavy Duty Cleaning
For situations where the blinds are heavily coated in dirt and grim, following these steps.

**Plastic blinds**
 1. Remove blinds from resting location.
 2. Fill a bathtub with water.
 3. Add a few drops of Dawn dish soap to the bathtub.
 4. Dip the blinds the bathtub for a few minutes, making sure not to submerge the top where the metal components are.
 5. Pin the blinds against the wall above the water in the bathtub. 
 6. Scrub the blinds gently with a thick-bristled brush.
 7. Rinse the blinds with a clean cup of water from the faucet. 
 8. Drain the bathtub. 
 9. Hand the blinds to dry outside or dry with a clean microfiber cloth.

**Other blind types**
1. Call a professional or replace blinds.

## Cleaning Frequency
These are suggestions for how often you should clean your blinds. 

**Once or twice a month**
 - Dust the blinds

**Once a year**
 - Deep clean
